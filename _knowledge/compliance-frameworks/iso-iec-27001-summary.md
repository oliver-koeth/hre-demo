# ISO/IEC 27001 summary

> Original summary. This is **not** the ISO/IEC 27001 standard text.

## What it is

ISO/IEC 27001 is an information security management system (ISMS) standard. It defines a management-system approach for identifying, assessing, treating, monitoring, and continually improving information security risk.

## Core ideas

1. Establish ISMS scope, context, leadership commitment, and policy.
2. Assess information security risks and choose risk treatment actions.
3. Implement selected controls and maintain a Statement of Applicability.
4. Operate supporting processes such as competence, awareness, documentation, supplier controls, incident response, and continuity.
5. Monitor performance through internal audit, management review, and continual improvement.

## Why insurers use it

- Demonstrates a structured security governance model.
- Helps map controls to privacy, operational resilience, and vendor oversight obligations.
- Supports auditability for regulators, customers, and business partners.

## Typical insurance focus areas

- policyholder and claims data protection
- identity and access management
- third-party administrators and outsourced service providers
- incident response and ransomware readiness
- backup, recovery, and business continuity
- secure development and change management

## Key deliverables often maintained

- ISMS scope and policy
- asset inventories
- risk assessment and treatment plan
- Statement of Applicability
- control procedures and records
- internal audit results
- management review evidence
- corrective action tracking
