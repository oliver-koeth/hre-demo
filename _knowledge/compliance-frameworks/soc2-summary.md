# SOC 2 summary

> Original summary. This is **not** the AICPA SOC 2 criteria text.

## What it is

SOC 2 is an attestation framework under which an independent auditor reports on controls relevant to one or more Trust Services Criteria: Security, Availability, Processing Integrity, Confidentiality, and Privacy.

## Core ideas

1. Define the system boundary, services, infrastructure, people, data, and vendors in scope.
2. Design controls aligned to the selected Trust Services Criteria.
3. Gather evidence that controls are suitably designed and, for Type 2, operated effectively over time.
4. Address exceptions, complementary user entity controls, and subservice organizations.

## Type 1 vs. Type 2

- **Type 1:** control design at a point in time
- **Type 2:** control design plus operating effectiveness over a review period

## Why insurers care

- Common customer and partner assurance artifact for SaaS, analytics, and outsourced platforms.
- Useful for validating third-party service providers that process policyholder, broker, claims, or finance data.
- Often complements ISO 27001, PCI DSS, and regulatory control programs.

## Typical evidence areas

- access reviews
- logging and monitoring
- change management
- vulnerability and patch management
- backup and recovery
- vendor management
- incident response
- HR onboarding and offboarding
